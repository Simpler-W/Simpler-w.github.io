English Words

Created2021-01-09Updated2021-01-09

abandon

[verb] UK /əˈbæn.dən/ US /əˈbæn.dən/

  • to leave a place, thing, or person, usually for ever
    We had to abandon the car.
    As a baby he was abandoned by his mother.
    We are sinking fast, and the captain gave the order to abandon ship.

  • to stop doing an activity before you have finished it
    The game was abandoned at half-time because of the poor weather conditions.

  • [abandon yourself to sth] to allow yourself to be controlled completely by a feeling or way of living
    He abandoned himself to his emotions.

aboard

[adverb, preposition] UK /əˈbɔːd/ US /əˈbɔːrd/

  • on or onto a ship, aircarft, bus, or trin
    Welcome aboard flight MH370 to Tokyo.
    We spent two months aboard ship (= on the ship).

absolute

[adjective] UK /ˈæb.sə.luːt/ US /ˈæb.sə.luːt/

  • very great or to the largest degree possible
    I have absolute faith in her judgment.

  • used when expressing a strong opinion
    He’s an absolute idiot!
    That’s absolute rubbish!

  • true, right, or the same in all situations and not depending on anything else
    Do you think there’s such a thing as absolute true/beauty?

  • An absolute ruler has unlimited power
    an absolute monarch

opinion

[noun] UK /əˈpɪn.jən/ US /əˈpɪn.jən/

  • a thought or belief about something or someone
    Waht is your opinion about the matter?
    Who, in your opinion, is the best soccer player in the world today?

  • the thoughts or beliefs that a group of people have
    Eventually, the government will have to take notice of public opinion.

  • a judgment about someone or something
    She has a good/high opinion of his ablities (= think he is good).
    I have a prettry bad/low/poor opinion of my sister’s boyfriend.

  • a judgment made by an expert
    It is my considerd opinion that he should be promoted.

monarch

[noun] UK /ˈmɒn.ək/ US /ˈmɑː.nɚk/

  • a king or queen
    The revolution overthrew the monarch.

consider

[verb] UK /kənˈsɪd.ər/ US /kənˈsɪd.ɚ/

  • to spend time thinking about a possibility or making a decision
    Do not make any decisions before yuo have considered the situation.
    We are considering selling the house.

  • to give attention to a particular subject or fact when judging something else
    If you consider how long he has been learning the piano, he is not very good.

  • to care about or respect other people or their feelings and wished
    Did you consider your mother an how she is going to feel about you leaving?
    She never considers anyone but herself - she is totally selfish!

  • to believe some or something to be, or think of him, her, or it as something
    We do not consider her to be right for the job.
    Do you consider him a friend of yours?

promote

[verb] UK /prəˈməʊt/ US /prəˈmoʊt/

  • to encourage people to like, buy, use, do, or support something
    I has long been known that regular exercise promotes good helth.

  • to raise someone to a higher or more important position or rank
    If I’m not promoted within the next two years, I’m going to change jobs.

overthrow

[verb] UK /ˌəʊ.vəˈθrəʊ/ US /ˌoʊ.vɚˈθroʊ/

  • to defeat or remove someone from power, using force
    The overthrow of the monarchy

  • the act of throwing a ball too far
