interview

[noun] US /ˈɪn.t̬ɚ.vjuː/ UK /ˈɪn.tə.vjuː/

  • a meeting in which someone asks you questions to see if you are suitable for a job or course
    I had an interview for a job with a developer.

[verb] US /ˈɪn.t̬ɚ.vjuː/ UK /ˈɪn.tə.vjuː/

  • to ask someone questions in an interview
    Who’s the most famous person you’ve ever interviewd on TV?

  • to answer questions in an interview for a job or course
    I interviewed for several jobs but I didn’t get any of them.
    He’s a good candidate on paper, but he didn’t interview well.

candidate

[noun] US /ˈkæn.dɪ.dət/ /ˈkæn.dɪ.deɪt/ UK /ˈkæn.dɪ.dət/ /ˈkæn.dɪ.deɪt/

  • a person or thing considered likely to receive or experience something
    Try this time finding a more suitable candidate.

course

[noun] US /kɔːrs/ UK /kɔːs

  • a set of classes or a plan of study on a particular subject, usually leading to an exam or qualification
    The first transition course has just begun.
    You take a three month course.
